Founded in 2011, GlobeMed at Rutgers University is part of a network of students in universities across the country who endeavor to create change in global health equity and social justice through partnership with grassroots organizations.

Our Partner

Change A Life Uganda

Migyera, Uganda

Our mission is to improve the lives of orphaned and disadvantaged children and their families in Uganda through quality education, healthcare and income-generating programs.

Our vision is to develop educated, healthy, and empowered Ugandan children as agents of positive social and economic change.

Visit changealifeuganda.org to get involved!

Education

Our programs aim to increase primary, secondary and university school attendance, leading to higher graduation rates. We do this by creating learning environments with improved student/teacher ratios, teacher training, safe sleeping arrangements and covered tuition for disadvantaged students. We also offer adult literacy classes to empower parents and help them become more engaged in their children’s education.

Healthcare & Water

Our programs address local health issues such as clean water, sanitation, HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, diarrhea, and various respiratory diseases affecting our families. In Migyera, we have successfully drilled a well and opened a healthcare facility, which begin to provide access to solutions for these issues.

Microenterprise

Poverty is widespread in Uganda and affects the ability to sell products and make a profit. Due to limited resources, feeding a family can be a daily struggle. Our microfinance programs bring opportunities to women and their families by helping them to develop marketable businesses and agricultural skills to establish and maintain financial independence and economic stability.
